Financial Services and Staffing account for a significant share of hiring demand.

The DFW market relies heavily on analysts to support operations, reporting, and performance tracking. The demand is business-driven rather than product-focused.

Candidates who understand metrics, reporting workflows, and decision support are well aligned with this market structure.



## Skill Demand vs Salary Premium

</p>

SQL appears most frequently across postings, confirming it as a baseline requirement.

Cloud platforms such as Azure, AWS, and Snowflake appear less often but correlate with higher average salaries.

The pattern is clear:

- SQL enables entry into the market.
- Advanced infrastructure skills increase compensation leverage.
- Depth of skill is more valuable than listing many tools.

## Strategic Career Positioning
The DFW market rewards candidates who:

- Demonstrate strong SQL fundamentals  
- Use Excel effectively for business reporting  
- Communicate insights through clear visualization  
- Develop cloud or modern data platform skills  
- Understand how timing and location influence opportunity  

This analysis connects technical skill development directly to measurable hiring demand.



## Assumptions & Data Notes

- The dataset does not capture every Data Analyst posting in DFW during 2023.
- Postings without salary information were excluded for consistent salary comparison.
- Some salaries reflect ranges rather than fixed compensation.
- Reposted roles may appear more than once.
- Skills were extracted directly from job descriptions and may contain repetition.
- Findings reflect 2023 data and may not represent current conditions.

These considerations were factored into the interpretation of results.
